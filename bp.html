  • Overview


    - Conducted UX research on the Helix navigation system. Helix was created as the central internal website for all things design related in bp.

    - Also worked on the design/frontend of the Digital Science Interns' group project.


  • Role


    UX Research , Information Architecture, Front End Development, UI Design.


  • Preparation


    This was my first time doing UX Research because I came from a computer science background.   

    - Asked UX Research employees at bp how they conducted their user interviews and how to pick questions to ask.

    - Recorded user interviews and asked UX Research employees for their feedback on how I could improve my interviews.


  • Picking Interviewees


    - Selected ten interviewees due to time constraints

    - Selected two representatives from each of the five design disciplines: product design, design engineering, content design, UX research, and design operations.

    - Half of the interviewees were new hires to bp’s design team and lacked prior experience navigating the Helix platform while the other half were experienced at bp.

    - Interviewees were also from different office locations: Chicago, Houston, and London.


  • Interviews



    - 30 Minute Interviews

    - Delved into employee’s history and background at the company.

    - Asked about software that is integral to their job.

    - Asked about the frequency of usage and their familiarity with the Helix website.

    - Sought suggestions on how to enhance Helix’s usability and benefits.



    Created a section for each interviewee and designated each of them a color so I could create an affinity map.



  • Research Findings


    - New employees had not heard of Helix, instead they would ask their peers for specific resources they needed because it was faster and more convenient.

    - Established employees had no use for Helix because they found the information on there to be "general" , they wanted more specific resources for their team.

    - Established employees wanted Helix to show statistics of design for interested clients. 


  • Tree Jack Testing


    Asked bp designers over monthly global design meeting to take tree jack test for Helix site.

    Tree Jack test contained questions on where to find certain categories on Helix Navigation.


  • Project Presentation


    Research findings were presented to the design team in the Houston office and were subsequently utilized to recreate the navigation system of the Helix site.


  • Biolens Group Project


    - Worked alongside 20 digital science interns

    - We were tasked with creating a project that connected bp employees with biodiversity on campus.

    - We created an app called Biolens where employees are able to take a picture of an animal/plant on campus and the app would identify what planet/animal that is.

    - I specifically worked on the design/frontend of the Biolens project using figma and visual studio code.


  • Constraints


    1. Although I had 12 weeks for the internship, I was handed my project later on. The timeline was changing as well as the requirements. With my limited time I was able to research with 10 participants.

    2. Prior to my internship I had really only studied computer science terminologies. Through my internship I came across many terms and practices that I was not familiar to. I learned a lot through my short time at bp.

    3. The Helix team I was working with was based in London. Being based in Houston it meant that I was only able to communicate with them in a small window of time. Half way through my internship the team was also replaced by contractors who were new to the company and needed to be introduced to Helix.


  • Reflections


    This experience has been instrumental in creating the foundation of my understanding of the design process and enhancing my design skills. My internship was a transformational experience because it helped bridge the gap between my background in computer science fundamentals and practices with those of UI/UX design.
