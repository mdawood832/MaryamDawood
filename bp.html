  • Overview


    I led user interviews and conducted detailed research, which directly influenced the redesign of bp’s internal design website's navigation.

    Played a pivotal role in connecting the Biolens team of 20 interns, bridging frontend development and design.


    Duration

    May 15th to August 10th, 2023

    Role

    Led UX research, optimized IA, and contributed to UI design and frontend development.

    Team

    Design Team, Biolens Team

    Tools

    Optimal Workshop, Figma, Mural, Visual Studio Code

  • Background


    - Helix is the internal website for all UI/UX Design disciplines in bp.

    - Helix had a mega menu and I was tasked with simplifying and improving the usability of the menu.



  • Preparation


    - Consulted bp's UX Research team on their interview methods and question selection.

    - Recorded user interviews and requested feedback from the UX Research team on how to improve my interviews.


  • Picking Interviewees


    - Selected ten interviewees

    - Selected two representatives from each of the five design disciplines: product design, design engineering, content design, UX research, and design operations.

    - Half of the interviewees were new hires to bp’s design team, unfamiliar with the Helix platform, while the other half had prior experience at bp

    - The interviewees were from various office locations, including Chicago, Houston, and London.


  • Interviews



    - Conducted 30 Minute Interviews

    - Explored employees' history and background with the company.

    - Inquired about the softwares essential to their role.

    - Asked about their frequency of use and familiarity with the Helix platform.

    - Gathered suggestions for improving Helix’s usability and effectiveness.



    Created a section for each interviewee and assigned them a color to build an affinity map.



  • Research Findings


    - New employees were unaware of Helix and preferred asking peers for specific resources, as it was quicker and more convenient.

    - Established employees found Helix unhelpful, describing the information as too "general" and preferring more team-specific resources.

    - Established employees also wanted Helix to display design statistics for clients.


  • Tree Jack Testing


    Asked bp designers over monthly global design meeting to take tree jack test for Helix site.

    The Treejack test included questions about locating specific categories in Helix's navigation.


  • Personal Project Presentation


    Delivered key insights to bp’s Houston and London design teams, driving decisions that directly impacted the Helix platform’s usability and design.

  • Biolens Group Project


    - Worked alongside 20 digital science interns.

    - Our task was to create a project connecting bp employees with campus biodiversity.

    - We developed an app called Biolens, allowing employees to photograph plants or animals on campus for identification.

    - I focused on the design/frontend of the Biolens project, using Figma and Visual Studio Code.


  • Reflections


    This experience laid the foundation for my understanding of the design process and significantly improved my design skills. The internship was transformative, bridging the gap between my computer science background and UI/UX design practices.
