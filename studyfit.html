  • Overview


    Study fit was created by students for students who juggle busy schedules and need reminders to prioritize their health. Exercise reduces stress, boosts energy levels, increases academic achievement, etc. Study fit offers a variety of exercises, including weightlifting, yoga, and cardio, to enhance the overall well-being of college and high school students.

    The prototyping phase involved semi-structured interviews, designing low and high-fidelity interfaces in Figma, prototyping interactions, and UX writing.

  • White Paper Research



    I wanted to dive into why students should focus on exercising while balancing their academic studies. I found that:

    - Higher exercise intensity is correlated with higher academic achievement

    - Sacrificing study time to exercise may have a positive impact on academic performance.

    - Having an accountability partner increases your chances of success by 95%.


  • Interviewees


    I interviewed 4 college students and 2 high school students to validate my initial hypothesis and investigate their pain points. The semi-structured interviews revealed students' challenges with balancing physical health and academic responsibilities.


  • Interview Script


    - "Can you describe your current physical activity routine, if you have one?"

    - "What are the biggest challenges you face in maintaining a balance between schoolwork and staying physically active?"

    - "Have you ever felt that exercising takes away time from your school responsibilities, or do you find it helps you manage stress?"

    - "How often do you feel too tired from schoolwork or studying to exercise?"

    - "Do you have access to resources (gym or fitness apps) to help you stay active? If so, how often do you use them?"

  • Voice and Tone


    I intentionally used all lowercase letters to establish a casual, approachable tone that resonates with a younger audience. The vocabulary was kept simple and conversational, creating a relaxed, non-intimidating environment for students to engage with fitness.
