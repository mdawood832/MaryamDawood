  • Overview


    Study fit was created by students for students who juggle busy schedules and need reminders to prioritize their health. Exercise reduces stress, improves mobility, and boosts energy levels. study fit offers a variety of exercises, including weightlifting, yoga, and cardio, to enhance the overall well-being of college and high school students.

    The prototyping phase involved semi-structured interviews, designing low and high-fidelity interfaces in Figma, prototyping interactions, and UX writing. The development was carried out using Android Studio.

  • Research


    I interviewed 4 college students and 2 high school students to validate my initial hypothesis and investigate their pain points. The semi-structured interviews revealed students' challenges with balancing physical health and academic responsibilities.


  • Personas



  • Pain Points


    - Time Constraints: Due to demanding schedules, students find it challenging to allocate time to work out.

    - Lack of Motivation: After long hours of studying, students find it hard to find motivation to exercise.

    - Knowledge and Guidance: Lack of information on how to properly workout makes it hard especially without the proper equipment.

    - Impact on Study: Students believe that exercising takes away time away from studying which might lower academic performance.


  • Problem Statement


    How might we help students balance their academic responsibilities with maintaining their physical health by integrating effective workouts into their study schedules?

  • Brainstorming User Flow


    I designed the user flow to reduce friction, making it easy for busy students to find and start a workout.



  • Project Goals

    - Design short, effective workouts that fit easily into students' schedules.

    - Encourage regular exercise among students.

    - Provide clear, easy-to-follow workout instructions and routines.

  • Voice and Tone


    I intentionally used all lowercase letters to establish a casual, approachable tone that resonates with a younger audience. The vocabulary was kept simple and conversational, creating a relaxed, non-intimidating environment for students to engage with fitness.


  • Design System


    - Green conveys peace and tranquility for students who are already stressed out by their hectic schedule.

    - Black represents power and formality ensuring that while the app is approachable, the design retains a sense of authority and seriousness the student's health requires.

    - The card design was used throughout study fit making it easy for students to scan relevant information.





  • Apple Watch Design


    - Allows students to sync their workouts with their Apple watch

    - Students can engage with study fit beyond the mobile screen, integrating fitness into their daily routines without added friction.

    - Students can easily track their progress and monitor health data (like heart rate) in real-time.
