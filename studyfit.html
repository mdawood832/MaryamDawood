  • Overview


    The motivation behind study fit stemmed from the struggle of students balancing their academic responsibilities, jobs, and home life with their physical health.

    study fit was created by students for other students who manage a lot during their day and need to be reminded to take time away for their health. Extended periods of sitting down crouched over a laptop finishing an essay over overtime can lead to various health issues. Exercise aids in alleviating those issues by reducing stress and anxiety, improving functional mobility, and increasing energy levels. study fit includes many forms of exercises, from weightlifting, yoga, and cardio aimed to improve the overall wellbeing of college and high school students.

    The prototyping of study fit included conducting semi-structured interviews, designing low fidelity and high-fidelity interfaces in Figma, and prototyping interactions in Figma. The development of study fit was done through Android Studio.

  • Research


    I went out and interviewed 4 college students and 2 highschool students to further validate my initial hypothesis and to investigate their pain points. The semi-structured interviews uncover student's pain points with their physical health and balancing school.


  • Personas



  • Pain Points


    - Time Constraints: Due demanding schedules, students find it challenging to allocate time to work out.

    - Lack of Motivation: After long hours of studying, students find it hard to find motivation to exercise.

    - Knowledge and Guidance: Lack of information on how to properly workout makes it hard especially without the proper equipment.

    - Impact on Study: Students believe that exercising takes away time away from studying which might lower academic performance.


  • Problem Statement


    How might we help students balance their academic responsibilities with maintaining their physical health by integrating short, effective workouts into their study schedules, thereby improving their overall well-being, productivity, and long-term health outcomes?
